Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was a renowned Indian civil engineer, statesman, and one of the most prominent figures in the history of modern India. He was born in 1861 in the state of Karnataka, and his contributions to the field of civil engineering have been monumental. Dr. Visvesvaraya was responsible for designing and constructing several important structures and projects across India, including the Krishnarajasagar Dam in Karnataka and the Mokama Bridge in Bihar. He was also a visionary leader and played a key role in shaping India's industrial and economic growth during the early 20th century. Today, he is honoured as a national hero and continues to inspire generations of Indians.



Early Life & Educational Qualifications of Dr. Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya In the Kingdom of Mysore, Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was born on September 15, 1861, in Muddenahalli village. His father's name was Srinivasa Sastry, and his mother's was Venkachamma. Visvesvaraya's early life was spent in his hometown, where he received his primary education. Visvesvaraya had an outstanding academic career, starting with his primary education at his village school. He then pursued further education at the Central College in Bangalore, where he obtained a bachelor's degree in arts in 1881. Later, he graduated from the University of Bombay with a degree in civil engineering in 1883. Visvesvaraya's educational journey didn't stop there. He pursued higher engineering studies at the College of Science in Pune, where he received a Licentiate in Civil Engineering in 1885. In recognition of his academic achievements, Visvesvaraya was awarded a scholarship to study engineering in England. He earned a Mechanical Engineering degree from the University of London in 1888. His educational journey is an inspiration to many and highlights the importance of continuous learning and dedication to academic excellence.
